## Principles and Philosophy

Coach Luka Hocevar is a widely recognized body transformation and fitness coach. He is the co-owner
and founder of Vigor Ground Fitness. He’s helped thousands personally and through his heavily

professional athletes to the average person looking to improve their quality of life and performance.
Hocevar got his start with kettle bell training as a professional basketball player in the early 2000’s. In
2004 he traveled to Denmark to learn from the great Pavel Tsatsouline, gaining invaluable knowledge
and experience. This program is a culmination of his experiences and results that he’s seen firsthand.
With the idea of building strength and muscle in mind, Hocevar also is a huge proponent of that strength

roots of his success, Hocevar has brought back philosophies and principles of training from the last two
decades, to help anyone wishing to achieve this kind of lifestyle. One of the wonderful things about this
program is the absence of equipment. It can be done with just one kettle bell and a small amount of
equipment. The joints will be of great focus here as well. The daily rituals that Hocevar will provide will
help with the health of the joints and only add more quality to your daily life.
You’ll be working out 3 days a week and all the bases will be covered here. From warming up, to joint
health, to strength, and conditioning sessions. It’s all here. This is a feasible, 8-week program that WILL
produce results. Hocevar will also provide progressions and regressions to help you tailor the routine to
your abilities but that will still challenge you and provide room for growth.
Longevity is the name of the game here. We want to build a solid foundation that will serve us for years
to come, not a fly by night program that will become impossible as we age and Hocevar has worked

## Daily Ritual

One of the great things about this program is that it not only promises the building of muscle,
endurance, etc. but also the adaptation of a healthier lifestyle. Hocevar would like to first introduce you
to the concept of “movement hygiene”. You see, when it comes to things like brushing our teeth and
other daily rituals that have become a part of our everyday life, we tend not to compromise. But we
don’t always treat our bodies in the same way. Hocevar has the perfect remedy for this with a daily
ritual dedicated to mindful movement of the body. He suggests that you try and work through this
routine sometime in the morning to help your body open up when it needs it the most, but you could
perform it at any time of the day. This routine will help you prep your body for the day ahead, your
training sessions, increase performance, eliminate nagging minor injuries, and reduce wear and tear,
providing you with more longevity and an overall improvement in your quality of life. Let’s get started.

## Neck Mobility

In a standing position, Hocevar turns his palms to face out
and extends his arms out to lengthen the tissues in the
neck region. He begins with a simple chin tuck, first pulling
his chin to his chest and then looking up at the ceiling. As
Hocevar works his way through the motions you’ll notice
he is not extending his neck outward but simply just
tucking his chin at the bottom of each repetition. Work
through 10 repetitions.
Next, Hocevar moves into some side to side motions. When
performing this one, he advises us to think about
looking as far back as we can behind us and
imagining that there is a string pulling the head
upward duing the movement. It’s likely one side
will feel a bit tighter than the other. This is natural
and it’s something you can work on as your
mobiity improves. 10 reps per side.

Lateral flexion is next with an ear to shoulder movement.
Hocevar gently moves from side to side, feeling the stretch in his
traps. You can play with this one a bit also, using slight rotations
to make the stretches more dynamic. Keep your trunk tight and
don’t allow your torso to tilt during the exercise. 10 reps per
side.

## You can also amplifiy the benefits a bit by using the hand to

create some tension. Here, Hocevar will hold the stretch,
aiming to go a bit deeper.
Once these initial movements are finished, Hocevar moves into the 360 degree rotation. Putting the
movements together, Hocevar first drops his chin to his chest, then raises it to his shoulder, followed by
tranistioning his gaze to the ceiling, and then through the same motions on the opposite side. Aim for
pain free movement here and start with smaller circles. You can always increase the range of motion as
things begin to open up! 10 reps per side.

## Shoulder Mobility

Moving on to the shoulders, Hocevar now adopts the same stance as
he did for the neck rotations but with his arms extended out in front
of him. He pushes his shoulders forward as far as his body will allow.
Notice there is no leaning forward here, only the extension of the
arms and shoulders.

After pushing the shoulders forward, Hocevar shrugs
them up to his ears. He then pulls them backward,
squeezing his shoulder blades together as tightly as
he can, before dropping them back down to the
starting position. Shoot for 5 reps in each direction.
Thoracic rotations are next up. Beginning with his arm
extended directly out in front of him and a hand on his ribs
to prevent himself from flaring in the torso, Hocevar
initiates the movement by lifting his arm upward. He’s
careful not to add a shrug at the top of the movement.

## Keep the elbow locked out and look to go as far as

possible. Range of motion will vary here from person to
person. At the top of this movement, Hocevar begins
to internally rotate his shoulder and looks to draw a
large circle with the arm. At the bottom of the
rotation, Hocevar’s palm is facing out and it’s time to
work through the same movement in reverse. He
takes the arm back as far as possible and begins to
rotate the hand back
to the inside as it rises
to the top of the
repetition.

Be sure to avoid rotaition of the upper back here as this is a bit of a cheat when it comes to the proper
movement. Keep your body locked in. If you get to a place that’s dificult, work thorugh it slowly,

each direction.
Next, Hocevar raises his elbows and positions himself
with his hands at his chest and his palms facing down.
With his glutes locked in, he begins to rotate from the
torso, going as far as possible with the upper back and
not allowing the hips to turn. When he’s reached the
furthest point, he extends his arm, opening his upper
body. He exhales strongly with the rotation and almost
pushes his hip forward at the top of the motion. Perform

## Cat/Camel

This superb spinal movement is common, and you may have seen it performed by simply dropping the
belly and arching the back but Hocevar adds a bit of a twist. Beginning on his hands and knees in an
arched position, Hocevar begins to imagine that there’s a thumb on each individual vertebra and with
his movement he is systematically pushing out each one individually. When he is fully arched. He begins
the same process in reverse until he achieves the camel position at the bottom. What’s different here?
Hocevar is mindfully moving each individual vertebra and this can take some time. Be patient and create
that mind muscle connection as you move through this one.
Now, adopting a child’s pose position, Hocevar elevates his
upper body and rests on one forearm. Keeping his forearm
connected to the ground, he puts his opposite hand behind his
back and opens his chest. He exhales with the opening and looks
as far as he can upward to get the full benefit of the twist. 8-10
reps per side.

## Spinal Roll + Spiderman Lunge

Beginning again in the child’s pose stretch, Hocevar elevates his hips

## Double Kettle Bell Squat 3-4 x 8

You’ll need to get the kettle bells into the rack position first

Kettle Bell Bulgarian Split Squats 3-4 x 8 Reps per Side
Before adding the kettle bells into the mix, Hocevar first positions himself so that he can balance
comfortably on the foot that’s connected to the ground. This will ensure that the focus is centered on
this front leg and that you’re not pushing too much into the elevated one. Now, with the kettlebells in
hand, Hocevar maintains a soft knee. Do not lock it out. He then sits back into the hip, keeping a hinge at
the waist and drops downward into the bottom of the repetition with control. Then with an exhale he
drives himself back to the top. Just to be certain that the proper leg is getting the attention, Hocevar
may even lift his elevated leg at the top of the repetition, to ensure there’s not too much weight in it.
